My Education

During the early years, I learned that I wanted to become a programmer. I was self-taught in C++, javascript, and mySQL. I learned by putting together different private servers that used these languages. I enjoyed coding the programs and seeing the results. With this background, I decided to study for a Bachelor of Science in Management Information Systems.

I attended Grossmont Community College for a short time period before transferring to Columbia College. While I was attending Grossmont, I was working full time at a restaurant. The scheduling errors constantly conflicted with the classes that I had. Since I decided to take in-person classes, it was difficult when the job did not follow the times that I was not allowed to work. For one of the classes I had only attended three out of sixteen class sessions but managed to pass the class.

I chose Columbia College because it was a military friendly college as well as accredited. When I decided to take this route, I made sure that I would be able to complete the full degree online since I was working two full time jobs to pay for college. Since I had half the credits required to finish my Associate's degree when I transferred into the college, I decided to take on two semesters with six classes each to be able to finish the Associate's degree by the time I arrived in Naples, Italy.

I made the decision to work towards preparing for the CompTIA exams. The preparation course tuition was covered through MyCAA's spouse career advancement. The courses would each be for eight weeks. I would be partaking in the courses at California State University San Marcos through online learning and virtual labs with virtual machines. I should be finishing with the courses by December 2020 and obtaining the certificates shortly after.
